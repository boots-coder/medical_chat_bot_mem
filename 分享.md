### **Mentorship Program Experience Sharing**



1. #### What ==**motivated**== you to join the Industry Mentorship Programme, and what were your expectations going in?

My primary motivation was to gain authentic industry and algorithmic experience. I expected this program to provide practical insights that would serve as a solid foundation for my future scientific research and professional career.



2. #### Can you share ==one key **takeaway or skill**== you gained from your mentor that you would not have learned in a classroom setting?

The most significant takeaway was the art of communication. My mentor, Ira, fostered an environment where I felt incredibly comfortable. This relaxed, peer-like relationship allowed me to freely express my ideas and innovative thoughts, which was ultimately the key to successfully building the entire project.

3. #### How did the programme ==**influence**== your understanding of your target industry or **career pathway**?

The biggest impact was the process of building a complete LLM-based (Large Language Model) dialogue memory management system from 0 to 1. It taught me to think and build from First Principles. In my subsequent internships, I have continued to approach projects by identifying the root cause or core need first, building a prototype, and then iteratively optimizing the framework.

4. #### In what ways did the mentorship experience help you ==grow== professionally or personally?

The greatest growth remains the improvement in my communication skills. Because Ira is so skilled at encouraging students to express their personal creativity and thoughts, it empowered me to complete the project more effectively. It transformed the experience from "working under pressure" into a journey of proactive creation.

5. #### Can you describe a **specific ==challenge==** you faced during the project and how your mentor guided you through it?

In the age of AI, tools like ChatGPT and Gemini have become our primary sources for technical skills. 

However, my mentor's guidance remained vital for a different reason. As a technical student from China, it is easy to fall into the habit of "working in silence" (burying one's head in work). 

My meetings with Ira pulled me out of this inertia. By constantly sharing my findings and discussing ideas, I found that new sparks of inspiration would consistently emerge through dialogue.





6. #### What made your mentor–mentee relationship ==**successful**==, and what advice would you give future participants to **maximise** the experience?

The most critical factor is courage. Regardless of your background, English proficiency, or technical level, the most important thing is to take that first brave step. 

Whether it is learning, thinking, or building a robust technical roadmap, every step of this journey requires stepping out of your comfort zone to face real challenges.

 My advice is to embrace that discomfort—looking back, the entire experience will become one of your greatest assets.
